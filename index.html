Security

Are purchases anonymous?

Each transaction is recorded in a public log(the blockchain: We’ll get to that shortly).Names of buyers and sellers are never revealed! While that keeps bitcoin users’ transactions private, it also lets them buy or sell anything without tracing it back to them.

Where are my coins stored?

In your wallet on the blockchain! Think of it like direct deposit to your bank from your work. No actual paper money is exchanged or even seen, rather a transaction history of money/currency transferred and your account with a specific, private ID that reflects the new value!

Still Wanna Know? FINE!

You computer can help solve or verify math problems’ answers within the community!

Who is providing the bit coins for solving these math problems?

Short Answer: Everyone in the mining community. Long Answer: When someone is mining a bitcoin, what he is actually doing is validating a bitcoin that has already been mined by another person. What this means is that when a miner named MinerJoe solves a complex math problem, MinerJoe does not get a bitcoin right away, the only way to trust that MinerJoe's solution is correct and NOT FAKE is to ask ALL OTHER miners to compute the same problem as MinerJoe's and see if his solution was indeed correct. Once ALL OTHER miners have successfully determined that the MinerJoe's solution was correct, MinerJoe automatically earns 1 bitcoin. No one GIVES MinerJoe that 1 bitcoin. MinerJoe AUTOMATICALLY gets the 1 bitcoin after his solution has been determined VALID by ALL OTHER miners due to the bitcoin mining PROTOCOL which is a decentralized architecture, that's what decentralization means.
